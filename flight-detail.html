Nepal boasts a network of domestic airlines that efficiently connect travelers across the country. Three major domestic airlines—Nepal Airlines, Buddha Air, and Yeti Airlines—alongside several smaller carriers, offer fast and convenient travel to major Nepali cities, towns, and points of interest.

Flight Types

Flights within Nepal are operated using two primary aircraft types:

Trunk Aircraft

  • These medium-sized commercial planes are similar to those used for short-haul flight routes worldwide.
  • Primarily employed for routes involving Kathmandu.

STOL Aircraft

  • Smaller, highly maneuverable aircraft capable of taking off and landing on shorter runways.
  • Typically used for flights to remote or mountainous areas.

Luggage Allowance

  • Standard Flight: 20-25kg of checked baggage + 5kg of hand luggage.
  • Mountain Flight (e.g., Lukla): 10kg of checked baggage + 5kg of hand luggage.

Check-In and Delays

We recommend arriving at the airport at least 90 minutes before your scheduled departure time. Domestic flights in Nepal are sometimes subject to delays due to weather or other factors, so patience may be required. If you're connecting with an international flight, it's advisable to allow a substantial buffer between the two to account for potential delays.
